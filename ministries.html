Ministries

  • Preaching

    Preaching is essential to vitality and health of the church. We are called to “preach the Word” because the Word is God’s means for salvation of the lost and growth of the believer (2 Tim 4:2; Rom 10:17). As we preach the Word of God, the glorious truth of the gospel reveals the face of Christ to us, which leads to a Christ centered, joyful life. At COHBC we are committed to faithful, expositional preaching of biblical truth. We preach through biblical books seeking to understand the truth so that it can be applied to our daily lives.

  • Music Ministry

    Music is an expressive way that we praise our God for all he is and for what he has done. Singing in the church is also a way we mutually edify one another with the truths of the gospel. At COHBC, we like to think our music is neither traditional nor contemporary, but Christ centered. No matter how old or new the song might be, or what musical style it is, we want it to reveal the glories of Christ and the wonders of His cross work for us. If you interested in serving in this ministry, please call Bob at 270-3961 or email him at bdelateur@cohbc.org.

  • Creative Serving Ideas

    Below is a list of serving ideas to stimulate you to love and good deeds:
    • Regularly invite someone over to your home for a meal.
    • Regularly write encouraging notes
    • Offer to baby sit for free so parents can go on a date
    • Encourage an elder / small group leader by sharing with them how you are spiritually encouraged by them.
    • Memorize a passage of Scripture each week with the express purpose of sharing it.
    • Create a prayer list of specific prayer needs of others and regularly pray for them.
    • Look through the church directory and call someone you have not seen at church in awhile.
    • Visit a senior and look for ways to serve him / her.
    • At a church event look for ways to help and offer assistance.
    • Help a young mother with her housework.
    • Regularly involve a single to be a part of your family's life.
    • Visit someone who is suffering with an illness and pray with them.
